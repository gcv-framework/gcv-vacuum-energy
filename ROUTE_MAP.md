## Strong-field compatibility note

The fixed-sector black-hole records are side/deep compatibility audits, not new core bridge steps. The exterior no-hair record states that, on the GR-exact GCV branch, one connected membrane-free fixed-sector exterior inherits the ordinary Einstein-Λ_eff exterior and does not allow the governed exact spacetime-constant vacuum mode to become smooth radial exterior hair.

The local-support baseline imports that exterior theorem and extends the black-hole audit to static spherical interiors: fixed Λ_eff contributes cosmological curvature, while local support is carried by the source-resolved Misner-Sharp mass and local-support tensor. A de Sitter-like core whose curvature differs from the fixed flux-sector value requires explicit local support, a shell, a membrane or sector transition, boundary/corner structure, or source-assignment-compatible strong-field dynamics. The exact spacetime-constant vacuum mode alone cannot be local black-hole substance or regular-core support.

The conditional sector-birth benchmark starts after that obstruction. It asks whether a compact finite-thickness local sector-boundary/corner source can be written without turning the exact constant vacuum mode into local black-hole material, without smooth parent-sector Λ(r) hair, and without leaking child-sector curvature or repayment into the parent exterior.

None of the black-hole records claims a new black-hole metric, a gravitational-wave detection of sector birth, evidence that any observed event formed a separate fixed sector, a singularity-resolution theorem, an evaporation analysis, an information-loss solution, a rotating Kerr-interior calculation, a full quantum-gravity endpoint, or a cosmic-cycle claim.
